Linear and Quadratic

Functions can take many forms with the first one displayed here being linear. Linear functions can be identified by their constant rate of change making them a straight line. The most common way to write a linear function is with slope intercept form (y = mx + b) where m is your slope and b is your y intercept. Next are quadratic functions which can be identified by their reversing curve known as a parabola. The most common form for quadratic equations is the general form (ax^2 + bx + c) where a, b, and c are real numbers.

Exponential and Logarithmic

Exponentials are third and they are identified in how they flatten out on one end known as a horizontal asymptote and rapidly increase at another. Their formula is (a*b^x) where a the y intercept and b is a value greater than 0. Lastly, logarithmic functions can be identified by an infinite increase on one end known as a vertical asymptote and level off on the other. The formula for logarithms is (log_b(a) = c) there b is the base that is raised by c to get a. Logarithms and exponentials act as inverses to one another meaning they are reflections of each other across the line (x = y).
